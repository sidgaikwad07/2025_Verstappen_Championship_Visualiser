# 🏆 F1 2025 Championship Story Visualizer
### *How Max Verstappen came within 2 points of the greatest comeback in modern Formula 1*

---

## The Story in Numbers

| | Lando Norris | Max Verstappen |
|---|:---:|:---:|
| **Final Points** | 423 | 421 |
| **Race Wins** | 7 | 8 |
| **Peak Deficit (VER)** | — | **-104 pts** (Dutch GP, Rd 15) |
| **Points Rd 16–24** | 91 | **216** |
| **Avg pts/race (Late Season)** | 16.25 | **23.88** |

Verstappen won more races, scored more points in the second half of the season, and averaged nearly 24 points per race in the final 9 rounds — yet still lost the championship by 2 points. This project tells that story through data.

---

## Championship Arc — The Three Acts

### Act I: The Slide (Rounds 1–15)
Verstappen started the season strong but never quite kept pace with a dominant McLaren. Piastri in particular was ruthless — averaging 20.6 pts/race through the first 15 rounds. After Spain (Rd 9), where VER scored just 1 point, the gap began to balloon. By the Dutch Grand Prix at Zandvoort, Verstappen sat **104 points behind the championship leader** — a deficit that looked fatal with 9 races remaining.

### Act II: The Turn (Round 16 — Monza)
Italy changed everything. Verstappen won in Monza, followed immediately by another win in Baku. He scored 216 points in the final 9 races — the highest of any driver over any equivalent phase of the season. Piastri meanwhile collapsed to just 11.2 pts/race over the same period, effectively removing himself from the title fight.

### Act III: The Final Charge (Rounds 19–24)
São Paulo gave Norris a 33-point sprint weekend and extended his lead to 49 points with three races left. Then Las Vegas: Verstappen 25 pts, Norris 0. Qatar: Verstappen 30 pts, Norris 18. Abu Dhabi: Verstappen 25 pts, Norris 15. The gap went from -49 to -2 in three races. It wasn't enough — but only barely.

## Setup & Running

### 1. Install dependencies
```bash
pip install -r requirements.txt
```

### 2. Launch the app
```bash
streamlit run app.py
```

Opens at `http://localhost:8501`. First run fetches 2025 championship data from the Jolpica API (~30s). All data is cached locally after that.

### 3. Telemetry pages (optional)
Telemetry charts require FastF1 data which downloads on demand per session. First load per qualifying/race session takes 30–120 seconds. The **Gap to Pole** full-season chart loads all 24 qualifying sessions — allow 10–20 minutes on first run, instant thereafter.

## Key Findings

- **Verstappen won 8 races** — more than any other driver — yet finished second in the championship
- His **-104 point peak deficit** (Dutch GP, Rd 15) is the largest deficit ever recovered to within 2 points at a season's end in the modern points era
- **Piastri's collapse** was the untold subplot: from 20.6 avg pts/race (Rds 1–15) to 11.2 avg pts/race (Rds 16–24) — he handed the fight to Norris
- The title was decided by **São Paulo**: Norris scored 33 points in the sprint weekend, building a 49-point buffer. Without that, Verstappen would have won the championship
- In qualifying pace (gap to pole), Verstappen was **statistically indistinguishable from pole position** across the final 8 rounds — the data shows the car and driver were genuinely the fastest package in the second half of the season
